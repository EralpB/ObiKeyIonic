FAQ 1) How can I use ObiKey? Go on a website, click "Login with ObiKey", and scan the QR code with your phone. Then you will see what permissions the website wants to access and have the option to generate a random identity, or use one of your previous identities. If you approve, your data will be sent through a secure channel to your browser. 2) Which websites are supported? You should either go to a website that supports ObiKey natively (they will let you know with a button, or a QR code), or download Google Chrome Plugin for ObiKey and use it in every website you visit. 3) What happens if I lose my phone? Your data is gone forever, but you are safe if you encrypted it with a good password. We are working on backup features, we need to prioritize the requests, so let us know what you want. 4) Is it dangerous if someone scans my QR code? No, the worst thing is that they could break your login process for that time, but they can't get your info in any way. 5) Which encryption are you using? We are using AES encryption with 256-bit one-time keys. It will take around 300000000000000000000000000000000000000000000000000(10^56) years to crack this. Your local data is encrypted with your password, so if you put a password like 123123 brute-force will be much more easier, but that's only an issue if your phone gets physically stolen, online transactions are always secured with random 256-bit keys, even if you don't put a local password. 6) Can I use with mobile websites? We support this kind of authorization, but developers need to implement this, it will take a bit time. 7) Can I use with applications? We support this kind of authorization, but developers need to implement this, it will take a bit time. 8) Can I store my credit card info?
