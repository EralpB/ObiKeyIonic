FAQ 1) How can I use ObiKey? Download "ObiKey Helper" extension for Google Chrome, then on a website click ObiKey extension icon and decide which permissions you want to give, and scan the QR code with your phone. Then you have the option to generate a random identity, or use one of your previous identities. If you approve, your data will be sent through a secure channel to your browser. 2) Which websites are supported? You should either go to a website that supports ObiKey natively (they will let you know with a button, or a QR code), or download Google Chrome Plugin for ObiKey and use it in every website you visit. 3) Can I move my data to my new phone? Unfortunately not yet, but we know how important this is. We are just trying to find the safest way. 4) Is it dangerous if someone scans my QR code? If this is the QR code generated by your browser, they can see your email, but if they see your screen that is probably the least they know. 5) Which encryption are you using? We are using AES encryption with 256-bit one-time keys. It will take around 300000000000000000000000000000000000000000000000000(10^56) years to crack this. Your local data is encrypted with your password, so if you put a password like 123123 brute-force will be much more easier, but that's only an issue if your phone gets physically stolen, online transactions are always secured with random 256-bit keys, even if you don't put a local password. 6) Can I use with mobile websites? We support this kind of authorization, but developers need to implement this, it will take a bit time. 7) Can I use with applications? We support this kind of authorization, but developers need to implement this, it will take a bit time. 8) Can I store my credit card info? Very soon, depending on the feature requests we will prioritize what we must improve. 9) I heard you were open-source?
